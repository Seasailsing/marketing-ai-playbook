## Marketing Frameworks & Workflows

### 1. Brand & GTM Messaging Process

How I build a consistent brand narrative across markets and audiences:

- Positioning foundation: audience, problem, differentiation, proof point
- - Message hierarchy: core message to market-specific adaptations to channel variants
  - - Localisation review: cultural fit check before any regional rollout
    - - Campaign brief: strategy translated into a shareable brief (Gamma for speed)
     
      - ### 2. AI-Assisted Market Intelligence
     
      - When entering a new APAC market or launching a campaign, I use AI to compress research time:
     
      - - Market sizing: Perplexity for rapid TAM/SAM estimates
        - - Competitive mapping: ChatGPT to structure competitive landscape
          - - Audience persona synthesis: Claude to turn customer interview notes into ICP profiles
            - - GTM narrative: Claude to draft localised messaging frameworks
             
              - ### 3. Enterprise Account-Based Marketing (ABM) Process
             
              - Coordinated, insight-led marketing for high-value enterprise accounts:
             
              - - Account selection criteria (revenue potential, strategic fit, relationship score)
                - - Personalised content tracks per vertical (Telco, Financial Services, Tech)
                  - - Multi-touch sequence: Thought leadership to Case study to Direct outreach
                    - - Measurement: Pipeline influenced, engagement score, deal velocity
                     
                      - ### 4. Integrated Campaign Operating Rhythm
                     
                      - A weekly cadence I run to keep marketing aligned with sales and product:
                     
                      - - Monday: Campaign performance review (reach, engagement, pipeline impact)
                        - - Wednesday: Cross-functional sync (Marketing, Sales, Product)
                          - - Friday: Weekly priorities update and next week planning

                            ## Prompt Library (Work in Progress)

                            Prompts I have found consistently useful in a B2B marketing context:

                            **Strategic positioning brief**

                            You are a B2B marketing strategist. Given the following company description and target customer, write a one-paragraph positioning statement that: (1) names the specific problem solved, (2) identifies who it is for, (3) articulates the unique approach, and (4) includes a proof point.

                            **Campaign performance summary**

                            Summarise the following campaign data into a 5-bullet executive update. Highlight what worked, what underperformed, and recommend one optimisation for the next cycle.

                            **Localisation review**

                            Review the following marketing copy for cultural fit in [market]. Flag any phrases, references, or tones that may not land well, and suggest alternatives.
